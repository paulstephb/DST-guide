Full Moon

The Glommer Ritual (Top Priority)

  • Glommer's Statue: Locate Glommer's Statue in the Birchnut forest before the full moon.
  • Get the Flower: During the full moon night, a Glommer's Flower will bloom on the statue. Pick it up immediately to make Glommer follow you.
  • Benefits: Glommer provides a passive sanity-restoration aura when you stand near him, and he periodically drops Glommer's Goop (an amazing fuel and fertilizer).

Chester's Transformation

  • Shadow Chester: Place 9 Nightmare Fuel inside Chester (one in each slot) before or during the full moon. He will transform into a shadowy version with 3 extra inventory slots.
  • Snow Chester: Alternatively, place 9 Blue Gems inside Chester. He will turn into a mobile fridge, preserving your food on the go.
  • Note: If Chester dies, he will respawn as normal Chester, and you will have to repeat the process during the next full moon.

World Changes & Farming Opportunities

  • Infinite Light: You do not need a torch or campfire during a full moon night. Use this free time to run around, chop trees, or mine rocks.
  • Werepigs & Monster Meat: Regular Pigs transform into aggressive Werepigs. If you feed a normal Pig 4 Monster Meats right before nightfall, it guarantees a Werepig transformation. Kill them for a 100% drop rate of 2 Meat and 1 Pig Skin.

New Moon

The Pitch-Black Darkness (Top Hazard)

  • Charlie's Threat: The night is 100% dark. Staying in the dark for more than a few seconds triggers Charlie (the Night Monster), who will attack and easily kill you.
  • Light Preparation: Always keep a Lantern, Miner Hat, or resources for a campfire/torch in your inventory. Do not wander away from base unprepared.

World Changes & Passive Buffs

  • Pig Immunity: Unlike the Full Moon, Pigs will sleep peacefully inside their houses all night and won't transform into Werepigs, making pig villages completely safe zones.
  • Sanity Management: Since the darkness is total, your sanity will drain faster if you don't stand directly next to a strong light source. Use Cactus Flowers or Cooked Green Mushrooms to keep your mind stable.

Autumn

Days 1 to 5: Exploration & Gathering

  • Map Out: Follow the roads or the coastlines to quickly reveal the map and find key locations.
  • Key Biomes: Locate the Savannah (for Beefalo), the Desert (for Cactus), and the Pig King (for Gold trades).
  • Basic Resources: Constantly gather Cut Grass, Twigs, Flint, and Berries along your way.

Days 5 to 10: Establishing the Base

  • Structures: Build a Science Machine immediately, then prototype an Alchemy Engine.
  • Equipment: Craft a Backpack, gold tools, and a Log Suit (or Football Helmet) for protection.
  • Food Supply: Build a Crock Pot as soon as possible.

Days 10 to 15: Winter Preparation

  • Vital Item: Craft a Thermal Stone before the cold weather hits.
  • Clothing: Shave Beefalos at night while they sleep to collect wool, and craft a Winter Hat.
  • Preservation: Defeat Clockwork monsters to get Gears and build an Ice Box (Fridge).

Winter

Days 21 to 25: Thermal Management & Food Shift

  • Stay Warm: Never leave base without your Thermal Stone (heated red next to a fire) and your Winter Hat or Beefalo Hat.
  • Ice Harvesting: Mine the mini-glaciers that spawn everywhere. Ice never spoils inside a fridge and acts as the perfect filler for your Crock Pot.
  • No More Berries: Berry bushes stop growing in winter. Switch your food source to Hunting (Koalefants, Spiders) and Fishing (if ponds aren't frozen).

Days 25 to 30: The Hunt for Blue Gems & Walrus Camps

  • The MacTusk Hunt: Find the Walrus Camps (active only in winter).
  • Key Drops:
    • Tam o' Shanter (Hat): The best sanity-restoring item in the game.
    • Walrus Tusk: Essential to craft the Walking Cane for a permanent 25% speed boost.
  • Blue Hounds: Be ready for hound waves; they now include Blue Hounds that freeze you upon death. Fight them near campfires or Beefalos.

Days 30 to 35: The Deerclops Arrival (The Ultimate Threat)

  • The Warning: Around Day 30, you will hear heavy breathing and deep growls. Leave your base immediately. Deerclops targets structures and will flatten your base in seconds.
  • Preparation: Make sure you have full sanity/health, fresh armor (Log Suits/Football Helmets), and a campfire ready nearby (away from trees so you don't start a forest fire).
  • The Fight: Deerclops has a massive sanity-draining aura and a freezing AoE attack.
    • Strategy: Hit him twice, then dodge his ice smash (Kiting). Alternatively, tank him next to a roaring campfire with multiple players wearing marble suits/helmets.

Days 35 to 36: Pre-Spring Cleanup

  • Deerclops Loot: Grab the Deerclops Eyeball. DO NOT EAT IT. Save it to craft the Eyebrella, the absolute best item for the upcoming Spring to immunity-check the heavy rains.
  • Ice Stockpile: Mine one last round of ice before it melts in Spring.

Spring

Days 36 to 40: Waterproofing & Rain Management

  • Stay Dry: Rain increases your Wetness meter. If it gets too high, your sanity drops, your tools slip from your hands, and you freeze.
  • The Ultimate Defense: Craft the Eyebrella using the Deerclops Eyeball from winter. It grants 100% waterproof protection and frees up your hand slot.
  • Budget Alternatives: If you don't have the eyeball, combine an Umbrella with a Football Helmet to reach 100% water protection.
  • Lightning Rods: Build at least one Lightning Rod at your base, or lightning will strike and burn your structures down.

Days 40 to 46: The Frog Rain & Aggressive Nature

  • The Frog Rain Event: A random spring event causes hundreds of frogs to fall from the sky. They are aggressive and will steal your inventory items if they hit you.
  • The Survival Trick: Do not fight them yourself. Lead the frog horde into a field of Tooth Traps or straight to a nearby Beefalo herd. They will clear them out, leaving you tons of free frog legs.
  • Beefalo Mating Season: Beefalo have red backs in spring. They are highly aggressive to anyone nearby. Wear a Beefalo Hat to safely walk among them.

Days 46 to 52: Moose/Goose Arrival (The Spring Boss)

  • The Nesting Grounds: Moose/Goose spawns near designated circular pond areas on the map. She lays an egg that hatches into aggressive spinning chicks (Moslings).
  • The Fight: She doesn't have a sanity-draining aura, making her the easiest boss. She will occasionally honk to disarm you; simply pick your weapon back up.
  • Key Loot: Kill her and her chicks to get Down Feathers. Save them to craft the Luxury Fan, which is vital for cooling down during Summer.

Days 52 to 55: Preparing for Summer (The Heatwave)

  • Ice Stockpile: Mine any remaining mini-glaciers before they completely melt away.
  • Flingomatics: Craft and place Ice Flingomatics at your base. In summer, your base structures will randomly catch fire due to the heat; these machines put out the fires automatically.
  • Endothermic Fires: Prototype an Endothermic Fire Pit (the blue fire pit) to cool yourself down once summer starts on Day 56.

Summer

Days 56 to 60: Heatwave Management & Fire Protection

  • Overheating: The heat will drain your health quickly. Stay cool using your Thermal Stone (leave it inside an Ice Box until it turns blue before heading out).
  • Endothermic Fire: Always build an Endothermic Fire Pit (blue fire) at your base to cool down. Carry resources to make temporary portable ones on the go.
  • Wildfires: Structures and plants will randomly catch fire due to the heat. Turn on your Ice Flingomatics to protect your base, or spend your summer underground in the **Caves** where wildfires don't happen.
  • Clothing: Wear your Eyebrella (it provides great sun protection) or craft a Floral Shirt to slow down overheating.

Days 60 to 66: The Oasis Desert & Antlion Tributes

  • The Sandstorm: The Oasis Desert is the safest surface biome in summer because wildfires cannot spawn here. Craft a pair of Desert Goggles to walk through the sandstorm normally.
  • Fishing for Blueprints: Fish in the Oasis pond to get crumpled packages. They contain the blueprints for the Desert Goggles and the Potted Fern.
  • The Antlion Tributes: The summer boss, Antlion, stays in the main desert. If you ignore her, she will cause massive earthquakes that destroy your structures. Give her tributes (Toys fished from the Oasis, or Gems) to delay the attacks, or fight her directly.

Days 66 to 70: Food Preservation & Cooling Tricks

  • Rapid Spoilage: Food rots 25% faster during summer. Rely heavily on your **Ice Box** and avoid harvesting food until the exact moment you need to cook it.
  • Luxury Fan: Use the Luxury Fan (crafted from Moose/Goose feathers) to instantly lower your temperature and extinguish any nearby wildfires in an emergency.
  • Refuel Flingomatics: Keep your Flingomatics fueled up until the very last day; late-summer fires are just as dangerous.
  • Gear Up for Year 2: Collect any remaining cactus flowers (they only bloom in summer) to make Summer Salads. Get ready, because once Day 72 ends, Autumn returns, and the entire yearly cycle restarts!
